Brand Guidelines

Everything you need to produce on-brand materials for the ITS Africa Conference, Cape Town, 9–10 March 2027. Use the assets exactly as supplied. You never need to choose a colour, font or layout: it's already decided here.

Cape Town 9–10 March 2027
01 · Logo

The logo

The "its" mark with the Africa Conference lockup. It comes in three layouts — landscape, portrait and square — and each layout is supplied in three versions: Standard, Transparent and Cut. Pick the layout that fits your space, then the version your background needs.

The three versions

Standard — the mark on a solid panel, supplied on white or on ink; drop it onto photos or colour where you need a clean plate. Transparent — no background; comes in black (for light surfaces) and white (for dark or vivid surfaces). Cut — the wordmark alone with the "its" mark knocked out; special cases only, never a substitute for the full lockup.

Landscape

Wide spaces: banners, email headers, website.

Portrait

Tall/narrow spaces: posters, roll-ups, stories.

Square / stacked

Social avatars, tight square placements.

Logo colour on each background

The mark is only ever black or white. Use black on light backgrounds (white, cream, gold) and white on dark or vivid backgrounds (ink, blue, green, terracotta, grey). Never set the mark in any other colour.

Do

  • Keep clear space around the logo: at least the height of the "i" dot on all sides.
  • Use the black version on light backgrounds, the white version on dark/photo backgrounds.
  • Scale it proportionally.

Don't

  • Don't recolour the mark — it is only ever black or white.
  • Don't put the black mark on a dark background or a busy photo.
  • Don't rebuild or retype the lockup — always use the supplied file.
02 · Colour

Colour palette

Drawn from the conference's African geometric pattern. Blue is the primary brand colour; gold is a highlight only. Click any swatch to copy its hex.

Do

  • Backgrounds: cream or white. Text: black or dark grey.
  • Use white text on blue, green or terracotta fills.
  • One accent colour per item — let the pattern carry the full-colour energy.

Don't

  • Don't set body text in gold on white (too low contrast); gold is for highlights only.
  • Don't use all six colours at once in a layout.
  • Don't put a colour behind body text.

04 · Pattern

The modular pattern system

A five-module geometric sequence inspired by Southern African visual traditions. The construction is fixed; colour moves through four coordinated roles to create 24 approved families without breaking continuity.

Use the pattern as a confident brand device: cropped as a band for structure, isolated into square modules, or full-bleed for atmosphere, with text always on a solid panel, never directly on the pattern. Always preserve its grid and sequence.

Canonical sequence · 1–2–3–4–5Continuous horizontal repeat

Colour roles

Cream and ink stay fixed. The four chromatic colours rotate through these roles, with every colour used exactly once in each family.

Lead fieldFlows from Module 1 into Module 2
Diamond accentPrimary focal detail in Module 1
Interlock AOpposing forms in Module 3
Interlock BOpposing forms in Module 5

The five modules

Modules may be cropped individually, but the full repeat always follows this order. Never redraw, stretch or change an anchor point.

The 24 approved colourways

The code reads Lead / Diamond / Interlock A / Interlock B. T = terracotta, Y = yellow, B = blue and G = green. Use one complete same-letter family at a time.

Do

  • Use the supplied SVG or PNG artwork.
  • Keep the 1–2–3–4–5 sequence intact.
  • Choose one same-letter colour family per composition.
  • Crop on the square module grid.

Don't

  • Don't recolour individual modules independently.
  • Don't mix suffixes unless a new seam has been approved.
  • Don't distort, rotate or move anchor points.
  • Don't place running text directly over a busy pattern.

06 · Email signature

Email signature

The signature uses the approved event logo, partner hierarchy, contact styling and blue-led pattern. Setting yours up takes about two minutes and needs no design work.

Open the signature page

How to set up your signature

Step by step

  • 1. Open the signature page (button above).
  • 2. Click Copy signature on that page. The whole signature is now on your clipboard.
  • 3. Paste it into your email settings (see right).
  • 4. Type over the name, role, phone and email with your own. Change nothing else.

The images load from this website, so they display correctly for every recipient. No files to attach or host.

Where to paste it

  • Gmail: Settings (gear icon) → See all settings → General → Signature → Create new → paste → Save changes at the bottom.
  • Outlook (new / web): Settings → Account → Signatures → New signature → paste → Save.
  • Outlook (classic): File → Options → Mail → Signatures → New → paste → OK.
  • Apple Mail: Mail → Settings → Signatures → + → paste.
